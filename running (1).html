My Story

Running found me before I really understood what it meant to be a runner. I joined the cross country team at Upper Arlington High School as a freshman, half out of curiosity and half because I wanted to push myself. What started as a challenge became a four-year love story with the sport.

I earned a varsity letter all four years in cross country, and competed in track as well. The early mornings, the long Saturday runs, the team camaraderie — all of it shaped who I am. Running taught me how to be comfortable being uncomfortable, a lesson I carry into everything from missionary service to accounting coursework.

The early morning track — where the best miles happen.

My Running Highlights

Here's a look at the milestones from my high school running career, in order:

  1. Freshman Year (2018–2019) — Joined the UAHS cross country team and earned my first varsity letter.
    • Survived my first 5K race in a downpour
    • Discovered I was better at distance than sprints
    • Joined the indoor and outdoor track team in spring
  2. Sophomore Year (2019–2020) — Improved my 5K time and began taking training more seriously.
    • Set a personal record in the 1600m during track season
    • COVID-19 cut the spring season short — a heartbreaking end
  3. Junior Year (2020–2021) — Returned to competition with renewed determination.
    • Led team warmups and became a vocal leader
    • Ran a strong cross country season in a challenging year
  4. Senior Year (2021–2022) — Finished my high school career proud and grateful.
    • Earned my fourth and final varsity letter in cross country
    • Competed in track & field one last time as a Bulldog
    • Graduated with the Cum Laude Society honor

Why I Run

🧠

Mental Clarity

Running clears my head better than anything else. A hard workout resets my perspective on whatever stress I'm carrying.

🤝

Community

The cross country team gave me some of my closest friendships. There's a unique bond built through shared suffering on a 5K course.

🏅

Discipline

Showing up every day, even when you don't want to — that habit has followed me into every other area of my life.

Gear I Love

A few things every runner should consider:

  • Brooks Ghost — My go-to daily trainer. Cushioned, reliable, and built for long miles.
  • Garmin Forerunner — Essential for tracking pace, heart rate, and distance on training runs.
  • Body Glide — A lifesaver on long runs. Enough said.
  • Nuun Electrolyte Tablets — Hydration made easy, especially in summer training.
  • Foam Roller — Recovery is half of training. This thing has saved my IT band more than once.
