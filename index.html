Why I run

Running, for me, isn't really about the running. It's about the twenty minutes where nobody can reach me, my brain finally stops sorting through everything at once, and I get to just breathe. Four years on varsity cross country in high school taught me discipline. Running as an adult taught me why that discipline actually matters.

Fun facts about running

A few things I've picked up along the way, organized by category:

  • The marathon distance comes from a legend, not a clean number
    • It's based on the story of a Greek messenger running from Marathon to Athens
    • The exact 26.2 mile distance wasn't standardized until the 1908 London Olympics
  • Running is one of the most efficient calorie-burning exercises
    • On average, a person burns about 100 calories per mile, give or take body weight
    • It's also one of the few sports that requires almost no equipment beyond shoes
  • Your mood really does change while you run
    • Aerobic exercise like running triggers endorphin release, sometimes called a "runner's high"
    • Studies link consistent running with lower self-reported stress and anxiety levels
