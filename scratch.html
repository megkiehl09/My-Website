Why I keep a separate page for this

The rest of this site is dressed up — a Bootstrap theme, a tidy navigation bar, a résumé meant for someone else's eyes. This page isn't that. I wanted one corner of the internet where I could write my own CSS line by line, the same way I lace up my shoes before a run: slowly, on purpose, with nobody watching.

Running, for me, isn't really about the running. It's about the twenty minutes where nobody can reach me, my brain finally stops sorting through everything at once, and I get to just breathe.

What's actually in my bag

An ordered list of what I grab before heading out, with the smaller details nested underneath:

  1. Shoes that have less than 300 miles on them
    • Currently breaking in a new pair
    • Old pair gets demoted to errand-running duty
  2. Something to track the run
    • Phone if I'm feeling social
    • Just a watch if I want to disappear for a while
  3. A playlist or a podcast
    • Music for short, fast runs
    • Talking heads for the long, slow ones
